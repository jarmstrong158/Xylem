# Xylem discipline

This session is running with the Xylem stack: a memory that persists, coordination
across agents, and a loop that compounds what you learn. Work the habit, not just the
task.

## The loop

1. RECALL before acting. At the start of a task, orient in the project's decision
   memory: read the active constraints and the decisions that touch what you are about
   to change. Do not re-litigate a settled choice, and do not silently violate one. Cite
   the ids you rely on.

2. CLAIM before editing. Before you touch files, survey the coordination board and
   check for conflicts on the paths you plan to change, then claim the task with its
   touches and branch. This keeps parallel agents (and your other devices) from colliding.

3. RECORD as you decide. When you make a non-obvious engineering or design choice,
   capture it the moment it happens: the problem, why this option won, the alternatives,
   the tradeoffs. Record standing rules as constraints. Future sessions inherit it.

4. RELEASE with an outcome. When the work is done, release the claim with a real note:
   what shipped, what was tested, the commit or PR, and the next action.

## Knowledge

Local distillation is automatic: at session end, Xylem captures this session's decisions
into the local knowledge store for you. Promotion to the team and to the org brain is
deliberate, never automatic - it goes through a recall gate, a generalization gate, and
an endorsement gate, one item at a time. Keep project-specific detail out of org-scope
knowledge.

## Tone

Recall, claim, record, release. Small, honest, compounding. The point is that the next
session - yours or a teammate's - starts smarter than this one did.
